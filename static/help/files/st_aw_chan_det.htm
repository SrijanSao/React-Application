Modifying Channel Details (Channel Details Panel)

Use the Channel Details panel to view details about the set of individual device traffic flows between Profiles (External Channels).

Each Channel on the Channel Details panel consists of the following:

  • A Profile or Node at the top (the provider of services) and a Profile or Node at the bottom (the consumer of services)

  • Traffic flows that are defined by either the name of the IANA service or the word Unassigned, a colon, and then the protocol and port that is used to listen to incoming data or requests

Note: If the ports and protocols are not listed on the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA) registry, they are prefaced by Unassigned. (This includes ports and protocols used by Stealth.)

To view the Channel Details panel and modify details of a Channel, do the following:

  1. From the map, select a Channel.

    A blue shadow appears behind the Channel and the Channel Details panel appears.

  2. If necessary, modify the policy associated with a traffic flow by clicking the policy icon and selecting a policy from the menu, as follows.

    Note: On a traffic flow that connects to two non-Stealth-enabled Profiles, the Clear Text Allow policy is set by default, it cannot be changed, and the policy is not visible.

    • On a traffic flow that connects two Stealth-enabled Profiles, set a Stealth policy: either Stealth Allow or Block Clear Text and Stealth.

      Note: Although you can set Clear Text Allow as an option, this is not recommended for traffic flows that connect two Stealth-enabled Profiles.

    • On a traffic flow that connects to one non-Stealth-enabled Profile, set a Clear Text policy: either Clear Text Allow or Block Clear Text and Stealth.

  3. Click the X in the upper-right corner to close the Channel Details panel.
